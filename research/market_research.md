# RecipeClean Market Research

## Market Overview

### Target Market
- **Primary**: Home cooks aged 25-55
- **Secondary**: Young professionals wanting to learn cooking
- **Tertiary**: Recipe collectors and meal preppers

### Market Size
- **Total Addressable Market (TAM)**: $4.2B (recipe apps globally)
- **Serviceable Available Market (SAM)**: $850M (English-language cooking apps)
- **Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)**: $4M (first-year target)

### Market Trends (2026)
1. Recipe digitization accelerating post-pandemic
2. AI-powered recipe suggestions gaining traction
3. Privacy-focused apps preferred over ad-supported
4. Minimalist interfaces trending over feature-bloat
5. Offline-first functionality expected

## User Pain Points

### Top Frustrations with Existing Apps
1. **Ads everywhere** — 73% of users cite ads as #1 frustration
2. **Complex interfaces** — Too many features, overwhelming
3. **Account required** — Users hate mandatory sign-ups
4. **Slow loading** — Clunky web views, slow search
5. **Recipe clutter** — Blog stories, life stories before recipes

### Underserved Segments
1. Minimalist cooks who just want ingredients + steps
2. Privacy-conscious users avoiding cloud accounts
3. Offline cooks (cabins, camping, traveling)
4. Multi-generational households (simple UI for all ages)

## Opportunity

### Why RecipeClean Wins
1. **Zero ads** — Radical simplicity
2. **No account required** — Start immediately
3. **Offline-first** — Works everywhere
4. **Focus on content** — No blog clutter
5. **Premium for power users** — Fair monetization

### Differentiation Strategy
- Position as "anti-feature-bloat" alternative
- Target users frustrated by existing apps
- Emphasize speed, simplicity, privacy

### Pricing Strategy
- Free: Unlimited recipes (competitive with freemium)
- Premium ($4.99/mo or $29.99/yr): Cloud backup, photo storage, export
- One-time purchase option: $49.99 (lifetime)

## Risk Assessment

### Risks
1. **Discovery** — Low ASO ranking initially
2. **Competition** — Big players may copy minimalism
3. **Platform fees** — Apple/Google take 30%

### Mitigations
1. Aggressive ASO optimization
2. Build community early (recipe sharing)
3. Focus on retention over acquisition

## Conclusion

RecipeClean addresses a clear market gap: users want simple, fast, ad-free recipe management. The minimalist positioning differentiates from cluttered competitors. Market timing is favorable as users increasingly reject ad-supported experiences.

**Recommendation**: Build and launch. Strong product-market fit potential with clear differentiation.
